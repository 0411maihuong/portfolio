01 — The Brief

Borrowing a language that isn't mine yet

The assignment asked us to study the visual language of a designer who shaped modernist graphic design, then reinterpret their principles through something local to us. I chose Armin Hofmann — his strict grids, high contrast, and refusal to decorate felt like the opposite of everything I was used to seeing growing up in Vietnam, which is part of why it interested me.

02 — The Concept

A lattice, a wordmark, a piece of bamboo

This poster was inspired by the visual language of the Independence Palace — its signature stone lattice pattern, a form of both balance and contrast, reinterpreted into a typographic wordmark. It also recalls the image of bamboo sections, a traditional Vietnamese symbol of resilience, flexibility, and cultural continuity.

The layout reflects the palace's architectural composition: balanced symmetry, strong hierarchy, and a structured grid that merges traditional symbolism with modernist clarity. By adapting architectural elements into graphic form, the poster explores how Vietnamese patterns that express identity and heritage can be bridged with the functionality and modernity of Hofmann's design principles.
